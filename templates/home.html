What are Dark Patterns?

Dark patterns are user interface design choices that manipulate or deceive users into taking certain actions that they might not otherwise choose to take. They are often employed in digital interfaces, such as websites and apps, to push users towards actions that benefit the company or organization behind the interface, rather than serving the user's best interests.

Types of Dark Patterns

  • Trick Questions: These are questions designed to confuse users or lead them to make choices that they didn't intend. For example, a website might use double negatives or confusing language to trick users into opting into services they don't want.
  • Hidden Costs: This involves hiding additional fees or charges until the user is further into the checkout process. Users may not realize they are being charged extra until it's too late to easily back out.
